## Overview

> Source: [dawson-2020a] — see [VUCA_BIBLIOGRAPHY.md](./VUCA_BIBLIOGRAPHY.md) for full citations and URLs.

VUCA skills are the sets of skills most needed for effective decision-making under conditions characterized by Volatility, Uncertainty, Complexity, and Ambiguity. Lectica has been studying and supporting the development of these skills since 2002.

### Skill hierarchy (idealized model)

```
META-SKILL ——— "VUCA Skills" (the entire collection)
   |
   |— MEGA-SKILL ——— 4 broad categories
   |     |
   |     |— MACRO-SKILL ——— groupings within each mega-skill
   |     |     |
   |     |     |— MINI-SKILL ——— sub-groupings (bold in skill maps)
   |     |     |     |
   |     |     |     └— MICRO-SKILL ——— can be practiced in context, in real-time
```

Categories are useful but not exhaustive. Boundaries are "quite permeable." The hierarchy is a useful tool that "simplifies the process of identifying micro-skills and helps us see how skills relate to one another." Real skills are far from orderly.

From a learning perspective, micro-skills are most important: they "can be practiced in context, in realtime" through micro-VCoL cycles.

### VCoL+7 (Virtuous Cycle of Learning)

The learning mechanism that drives all skill development:

```
SET GOAL → GATHER INFORMATION → APPLY → REFLECT → (new goal)
```

The +7 supporting skills:

1. Reflectivity
2. Self-awareness / self-monitoring
3. Seeking and evaluating information
4. Making connections
5. Applying knowledge
6. Seeking and working with feedback
7. Recognizing and managing bias

"Applied regularly, VCoL has been shown to accelerate mental development and improve thinking skills."

### Micro-VCoLing

A miniature, targeted, contextual learning cycle focused on one micro-skill. Introduced in 2019, micro-VCoLing and skill mapping drove average VUCA scores from 47 (2014-2018) to 55 (2019-2024) — an 8-point increase.

## Mega-skill 1: Collaborative Capacity [dawson-2020b]

**Role:** The most foundational of all VUCA skills. Collaborative capacity skills "either support the development of other VUCA skills or are called upon when practicing other VUCA skills."

**Definition:** The ability to work cooperatively with others in a variety of contexts, including collaborative learning, creative thinking, problem-solving, and decision-making.

### Macro-skill 1.1: Self-regulation

The ability to manage our own emotions and behavior.

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Cultivating openness:

- Cultivating a healthy active observer (fundamental micro-skill — present in virtually every micro-VCoL exercise)
- Actively seeking new information and perspectives
- Willingness to revise own views based on new evidence

Self-awareness and humility:

- Recognizing own limits and biases
- Acknowledging uncertainty and not-knowing
- Distinguishing what I know from what I think I know

Regulating behavior and emotions:

- Pausing before reacting in emotionally charged situations
- Separating emotional reaction from analytical evaluation
- Maintaining constructive tone under pressure

Combating bias:

- Identifying confirmation bias
- Actively seeking disconfirming evidence
- Recognizing groupthink

### Macro-skill 1.2: Perspective-seeking

The act of seeking information or ideas from other individuals or sources with the goal of enhancing knowledge, building group knowledge, working together, or making collective decisions. "Actively seeking the perspectives of others is essential for individual development and all cooperative human activity."

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Clarifying understanding:

- Asking open-ended questions
- Paraphrasing to verify comprehension
- Active listening without premature judgment

Building trust and establishing rapport:

- Creating psychologically safe space
- Consistent behavior and keeping commitments
- Appropriate vulnerability sharing

Cultivating other-awareness:

- Attention to nonverbal signals
- Considering the context and position of others
- Recognizing emotional states of counterparts

### Macro-skill 1.3: Perspective-taking

A mental process in which we attempt to see a situation or idea from another individual's or group's perspective. "It is best done after we have developed a reasonable understanding of a perspective through perspective-seeking."

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Mental simulation of another's viewpoint:

- Imagining the situation from a different angle
- Identifying motivations and concerns of the other party
- Distinguishing between perspective and agreement

### Macro-skill 1.4: Communication

Skills that increase collaborative capacity through mutual understanding, negotiation, and alignment.

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Fostering mutual understanding:

- Translating between stakeholder languages (technical, legal, business)
- Adapting communication style to audience
- Verifying message was understood as intended

Negotiating compromises:

- Identifying non-negotiable boundaries vs. flexible positions
- Seeking win-win solutions
- Separating positions from interests

Aligning around shared goals:

- Formulating shared goals from conflicting positions
- Building commitment to collective decisions

### Key insight about collaborative capacity

Some skills, such as perspective-seeking, are "ubiquitous. They show up repeatedly in maps focused on a variety of meta and mega-skills." These are more general and fundamental than other skills. "They are often skills we can benefit from practicing continuously throughout our lives."

## Mega-skill 2: Perspective Coordination [dawson-2020c]

**Role:** Builds upon and extends collaborative capacity skills. "Wheels within wheels" — building one area of skill inevitably develops others.

**Definition:** A dynamic set of skills that supports human interactions by fostering mutual respect, nurturing creativity, expanding minds, generating and developing ideas, leveraging conflict, and supporting healthy relationships.

"Robust collaborative capacity skills are a prerequisite for the development of perspective coordination skills, but you will also find that practices for building perspective coordination skills build collaborative capacity skills."

### Macro-skill 2.1: Identifying relevant perspectives

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Consulting stakeholders:

- Mapping all affected parties
- Identifying missing voices
- Actively reaching out to underrepresented groups

Evaluating sources:

- Assessing credibility and relevance of sources
- Distinguishing fact from opinion
- Identifying potential biases in sources

Consulting trusted sources:

- Identifying domain experts
- Cross-referencing between independent sources

### Macro-skill 2.2: Supporting productive interactions

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Cultivating respectful interactions:

- Active listening without interrupting
- Validating perspectives even in disagreement
- Constructive feedback

Facilitating generative conversations:

- Managing group dynamics
- Preventing single-voice dominance
- Maintaining focused conversation
- Redirecting destructive patterns

### Macro-skill 2.3: Integrating perspectives

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Organizing information:

- Structuring opposing viewpoints into coherent overview
- Categorizing arguments by type (factual, values-based, procedural)

Identifying and working with tensions:

- Naming opposing positions without judgment
- Finding root causes of disagreements
- Distinguishing real conflicts from misunderstandings

Finding common ground:

- Identifying shared values and goals
- Building on areas of agreement

Conflict resolution techniques:

- Structured methods for resolving deadlocks
- Escalation mechanisms

### Key insight about perspective coordination

"Because complex skills often integrate multiple precursor skills, they can be treated as micro-skills by a person who has built those precursor skills, but must be treated as macro or mini-skills by a person who has not built the precursor skills." The same skill represents different levels of challenge for different people.

## Mega-skill 3: Contextual Thinking [dawson-2020d]

**Role:** Emergent mega-skill. "The development of contextual thinking skills is even more immediately and explicitly connected to Lectical Level (mental complexity)" than other VUCA skills. Development hinges on consistent exercise of collaborative capacity and perspective coordination skills.

**Definition:** A set of interrelated skills that increase the scope, depth, and accuracy of understanding of issues, problems, or conflicts. "Whereas collaborative capacity and perspective coordination skills function as the engines of development, robust contextual thinking skills are more emergent."

**Empirical finding:** "The statistical correlation between Lectical Scores and contextual thinking scores is stronger than the correlation between Lectical Scores and any other VUCA skill."

### Macro-skill 3.1: The situation

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Defining the current state:

- Precise description of current state without evaluative judgments
- Identifying key variables of the situation
- Distinguishing symptoms from causes
- Mapping causal relationships

Filling knowledge gaps:

- Explicitly identifying "what I don't know"
- Systematically collecting missing information
- Distinguishing important gaps from unimportant ones
- Prioritizing gaps by impact on decisions

### Macro-skill 3.2: The larger context

"All situations arise in a context. Contexts can be historical, hierarchical, broad, narrow, near, or distant. They can involve individuals, groups, or entities and can be intentional or emergent. They can play causal or mediating roles, and can both influence and be influenced by the choices we make."

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Determining relevance of contexts:

- Assessing which contexts matter for the situation at hand
- Distinguishing near and distant contexts
- Identifying causal vs. mediating roles of contexts

Exploring historical drivers:

- Identifying historical precedents
- Recognizing recurring patterns
- Understanding how the current situation evolved

Identifying and leveraging historical exemplars:

- Finding analogies in other domains or periods
- Critically evaluating relevance of analogies

Accounting for hierarchical contexts:

- Understanding superordinate systems (industry, regulatory framework, society)
- Identifying how higher-level contexts influence the situation
- Recognizing feedback loops between levels

Accounting for broader contexts:

- Industry, regional, global influences
- Emergent vs. intentional contexts
- Cultural and values contexts

### Macro-skill 3.3: Constraints and affordances

"All contexts are accompanied by constraints and affordances. We make better decisions when we identify these and take into account their potential effects."

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Constraints imposed by values:

- Identifying value-based limits of organizations and individuals
- Recognizing implicit vs. explicit values

Constraints imposed by objectives:

- Mapping conflicting objectives
- Prioritizing goals

Constraints imposed by conventions and rules:

- Regulatory constraints (laws, regulations, standards)
- Organizational norms and processes
- Industry standards and expectations

Constraints imposed by resources:

- Budget, people, time, competencies
- Technological infrastructure
- Capacity for change

Identifying affordances (opportunities):

- What does the current situation enable?
- Which constraints are actually assumptions that can be challenged?
- Unexpected opportunities arising from constraints

### Key insight about contextual thinking and Lectical Level

The argument connecting contextual thinking to mental complexity:

1. Practicing collaborative capacity and perspective coordination micro-skills increases mental complexity by bringing in high-quality information that creates, reinforces, or prunes connections in the brain.
2. The result is a dynamic and constantly developing mental network.
3. Our current mental networks determine how we see and interpret the world.
4. When looking at a particular situation, we see it through the lens of our mental network. The more complex the network, the more we can see.
5. Situations are embedded in larger contexts. As mental networks increase in complexity, we can see more contexts and see them more comprehensively.
6. An individual's ability to see or understand a particular context is dependent on the match between their mental complexity and the complexity of the context.

Practical implication: "Curricula built around skills do a better job supporting optimal learning than those that are built around content precisely because they allow diverse learners to develop in their own 'Goldilocks zones,' rather than insisting that everyone learns exactly the same content at the same age or moment in time."

## Mega-skill 4: Decision-Making Process [dawson-2021a]

**Role:** Most neglected mega-skill. The only VUCA skill map with a mostly temporal arrangement. Leverages and extends all three previous mega-skills.

**Definition:** Skills called upon during decision-making processes. Most are also manifestations of VCoL+7 skills.

**Empirical finding:** "Thousands of Lectical Assessments have revealed that the single most neglected mega-skill in the decision-making process map is deciding itself. Few adults are able to explain in any detail how they actually make a decision. Most of us rely primarily on intuition."

### Macro-skill 4.1: Framing

"By default, all decision-making begins with a frame of reference."

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Problem definition:

- Formulating the problem as a question, not a pre-assumed solution
- Identifying assumptions in the framing
- Considering alternative framings of the same problem
- What is at stake? For whom? In what timeframe?

Identifying key considerations:

- Determining what matters most
- Recognizing tradeoffs inherent in the framing

Recognizing gaps and biases:

- What information is missing for quality decision-making?
- What biases may influence the framing?

### Macro-skill 4.2: Setting goals

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Identifying desired outcomes:

- Explicit formulation of success criteria
- Distinguishing minimum from ideal outcomes

Seeking agreement on goals:

- Aligning goals across stakeholders
- Resolving goal conflicts

### Macro-skill 4.3: Gathering information

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Considering diverse perspectives:

- Actively seeking opposing viewpoints
- Ensuring all relevant groups are represented

Evaluating validity of information:

- Assessing quality of evidence
- Distinguishing correlation from causation

Recognizing biases in sources:

- Identifying conflicts of interest
- Considering motivations of information sources

### Macro-skill 4.4: Identifying solutions

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Generating alternatives:

- Brainstorming without premature evaluation
- Considering unusual approaches
- Combining elements from different alternatives

Considering ethical implications:

- Impacts on different groups
- Short-term vs. long-term consequences
- Precedent-setting effects of the decision

### Macro-skill 4.5: Deciding

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Setting the appropriate level of collaboration:

- Who decides? (individual, team, consensus, authority)
- What is the role of advisors vs. decision-makers?

Getting clear about the specifics of the process:

- What criteria have what weight?
- What is the fallback if consensus is not reached?
- How will the decision be documented?

### Macro-skill 4.6: Implementing

**Mini-skills and micro-skills:**

Setting milestones and delegating roles:

- Who does what by when?
- What are the dependencies between steps?

Making contingency plans:

- What if a key assumption doesn't hold?
- What are the escalation mechanisms?

Measuring outcomes:

- How will we know the decision was correct?
- When and how to revisit the decision?

## Lectical Levels (measurement scale) [fischer-1980], [lectica-levels]

The Lectical Scale is a refinement of Kurt Fischer's Skill Scale — a developmental scale from birth to the highest levels of development. It measures increasing complexity, not content knowledge or "right answers."

### Scale structure

Scores are represented at three levels of precision:

- **Whole-level scores:** e.g., "Adults typically perform in levels 10 and 11"
- **Zone scores:** Half-level (e.g., advanced linear thinking, early systems thinking, advanced systems thinking)
- **Phase scores:** Quarter-level (e.g., 11a = 1100-1124, 11b = 1125-1149, 11c = 1150-1174, 11d = 1175-1199)

Phases represent degree of elaboration within a level:

- a = transitional
- b = unelaborated
- c = elaborated
- d = highly elaborated

### Adult-relevant levels

| Level | Name | Description | Example decision-making |
|-------|------|-------------|------------------------|
| 9 | Single Abstractions | Concrete concepts becoming abstract | Simple either/or reasoning |
| 10 | Abstract Mappings | Coordinating abstractions linearly | Weighing alternatives, sequential logic |
| 11 | Abstract Systems | Systems of abstract mappings | Systemic thinking, coordinating system elements |
| 12 | Single Principles | Coordinating abstract systems into principles | Principled reasoning (rare) |
| 13 | Principles Mappings | Coordinating multiple principles | (extremely rare) |

Adult average: ~1097 (level 10b, advanced linear thinking zone)

## Empirical data [dawson-2024a], [dawson-2024b]

> Detailed empirical data with gap analysis is in [VUCA_EMPIRICAL_DATA.md](./VUCA_EMPIRICAL_DATA.md).

### VUCA scores of leaders (n=2,193, from LDMA assessments)

| Mega-skill | Mean score (0-100) |
|---|---|
| Perspective coordination | 47 |
| Decision-making process | 42 |
| Collaborative capacity | 37 |
| Contextual thinking | 36 |

Ideal scores for senior leaders: ~85 for all four. Current averages are approximately half of ideal.

### VUCA score trends over time (n=4,296 LDMAs, 2008-2024)

| Period | Mean VUCA Score | Key change |
|--------|----------------|------------|
| 2009-2013 | 41 | Baseline |
| 2014-2018 | 47 (+6) | Shift to VCoLing emphasis in courses and reports |
| 2019-2024 | 55 (+8) | Introduction of micro-VCoLing and skill mapping |

The predictive power of management level over VUCA scores increased from 3.0% (2009-2013) to 4.5% (2014-2018) to 13.1% (2019-2024), meaning certified consultants are "helping leaders build VUCA skills that are increasingly commensurate with the task demands of their roles."

## The complexity gap [dawson-nd-a], [dawson-nd-b]

"The complexity gap is the distance between the complexity level of our thinking and the complexity of the issues confronted in our personal and work lives." Role complexity increases linearly with organizational hierarchy, but cognitive complexity of leaders does not keep pace. The gap widens at senior levels. AI governance adds additional layers of complexity (regulatory, ethical, technological) that deepen this gap.

Two ways to bridge the gap: (1) learn optimally to increase complexity and quality of thinking, and (2) develop VUCA skills that allow collaboration and perspective coordination to tackle problems too complex for any single individual.

## Assessment tools (reference only — Lectica proprietary) [lectica-org]

**LDMA (Lectical Decision Making Assessment)** — Flagship leadership assessment. Presents workplace dilemma, measures three dimensions: Lectical Score (complexity), Clarity Score (argumentation quality, 0-100), VUCA Score (four mega-skills, 0-100 each). Developed 2002-2006 with US intelligence community (CIA, NSA). Proven to double developmental growth rate when embedded in development programs.

**LRJA (Lectical Reflective Judgment Assessment)** — Measures reflective judgment. Targets CAOS skills (Competing ideas, Accelerating change, information Overload, Social division).

**CLAS (Computerized Lectical Assessment System)** — Automated scoring using Lectical Dictionary (hundreds of thousands of meaning units mapped to developmental phases). Based on human analysis of 50,000+ assessments.

**Lectica First** — Recruitment screening. Compares candidate mental skills to role complexity.

**Team Fit Snapshot** — Assesses developmental compatibility within teams.

**Validity:** Inter-rater agreement 80-97%. No gender or ethnic biases demonstrated. Stable on retest. Domain-general (same criteria across any subject matter).

## Key principles for application

1. **Skills develop only through practice.** Practice always happens at the micro level through micro-VCoLing.
2. **Each individual automatically practices at their own growth edge.** The same micro-skill can be assigned to a diverse group — each person develops in their own "Goldilocks zone."
3. **Mental complexity constrains skill performance.** We automatically practice at our current growth edge.
4. **All micro-skills can be practiced at multiple Lectical Levels.** Complexity of understanding and learning are affected by mental complexity, but the skill can still be practiced.
5. **Many skills don't have a clear point of mastery.** They continue to develop throughout our lives.
6. **A young child can and should practice skills that take many years to develop.** Current limitations form essential foundations for future development.
7. **Contextual thinking is the most complexity-dependent mega-skill.** It is also typically the weakest (mean leader score: 36/100).
8. **Decision-making is the most neglected mega-skill.** Most adults cannot describe how they make decisions.
9. **Skill maps are never finished.** They are useful but imprecise tools with permeable boundaries.
