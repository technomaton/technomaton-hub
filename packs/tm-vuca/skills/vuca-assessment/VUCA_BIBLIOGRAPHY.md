## Source Abstracts

### [dawson-2020a] VUCA unpacked (1) — Introduction

Introduces the VUCA skills meta-framework. Defines the five-level skill hierarchy (META → MEGA → MACRO → MINI → MICRO). Introduces VCoL+7 (Virtuous Cycle of Learning) as the core learning mechanism. Establishes that micro-skills are the actionable units of development — they "can be practiced in context, in real-time." Sets the foundation for the four subsequent articles unpacking each mega-skill.

### [dawson-2020b] VUCA unpacked (2) — Collaborative capacity

Unpacks the first and most foundational mega-skill. Collaborative capacity skills "either support the development of other VUCA skills or are called upon when practicing other VUCA skills." Covers four macro-skills: Self-regulation, Perspective-seeking, Perspective-taking, and Communication. Details mini-skills and micro-skills within each, including cultivating openness, building trust, active listening, and adapting communication to audience.

### [dawson-2020c] VUCA unpacked (3) — Perspective coordination

Details the second mega-skill: integrating information from multiple sources and viewpoints. Covers three macro-skills: Information gathering, Integrating diverse perspectives, and Cross-validating information. Emphasizes that perspective coordination is not just hearing different views but actively synthesizing them into a coherent understanding.

### [dawson-2020d] VUCA unpacked (4) — Contextual thinking

Details the third mega-skill and the most complexity-dependent of all four. Covers three macro-skills: Situational awareness, Considering broader context, and Recognizing constraints and interconnections. Empirically the weakest dimension (mean leader score: 36/100). The article shows why senior roles demand systems-level contextual thinking that most leaders haven't developed.

### [dawson-2021a] VUCA unpacked (5) — Decision-making process

Unpacks the fourth and most neglected mega-skill. Six macro-skills: Framing the problem, Setting goals, Gathering and evaluating information, Identifying solutions, Deciding, and Implementing. Key insight: most adults cannot describe how they make decisions — the process is implicit and unexamined. Published January 2021 (not 2020 like parts 1-4).

### [dawson-2024a] VUCA skills are on the rise!

Presents longitudinal trend data from n=4,296 LDMA assessments (2008-2024). Shows three periods: baseline (2009-2013, mean 41), VCoLing era (2014-2018, mean 47, +6), and micro-VCoLing era (2019-2024, mean 55, +8). Reports that predictive power of management level increased from 3.0% to 13.1% R², indicating consultants are helping leaders build role-commensurate skills.

### [dawson-2024b] Leader decisions part 1: How good are Leaders' VUCA skills?

Presents cross-sectional data from n=2,193 LDMA assessments. Reports mean scores by mega-skill: Perspective coordination 47, Decision-making 42, Collaborative capacity 37, Contextual thinking 36 (all out of 100). Establishes that current averages are approximately half of ideal senior leader scores (~85).

### [dawson-2024c] VUCA skills & management levels

Maps VUCA score targets to management hierarchy: entry-level ~55, mid-level ~70, senior ~85, CEO ~90-100. Key finding: even CEOs perform in the 55-60 range, "well below the 90-100 range that would serve them best."

### [dawson-nd-a] VUCA skills, VCoL, & the complexity gap

Connects the complexity gap concept to VUCA skills and the VCoL learning mechanism. Shows how micro-VCoLing specifically addresses the gap by providing structured practice at the growth edge. Links individual skill development to organizational capacity for handling complex problems.

### [dawson-nd-b] The complexity gap

Defines the complexity gap: "the distance between the complexity level of our thinking and the complexity of the issues confronted in our personal and work lives." Explains that role complexity increases linearly with hierarchy but cognitive complexity does not keep pace. Gap widens at senior levels. Two bridging strategies: (1) learn optimally, (2) develop collaborative VUCA skills.

### [fischer-1980] A theory of cognitive development

Foundational theoretical work proposing the Dynamic Skill Theory. Establishes the hierarchical skill scale from reflexes through principles, showing how cognitive development proceeds through increasingly complex coordinations. The Lectical Scale is a refinement of Fischer's Skill Scale. Published in *Psychological Review*, 87(6), 477-531.

### [lectica-org] Lectica, Inc.

Research and assessment organization founded by Theo Dawson. Develops and administers the Lectical Assessment System. Has studied and supported VUCA skill development since 2002. Provides LDMA, LRJA, CLAS, Lectica First, and Team Fit Snapshot assessments.

### [lectica-levels] Lectical Levels & Benchmarks

Reference page defining the Lectical Scale structure: whole-level, zone (half-level), and phase (quarter-level) precision. Documents adult-relevant levels 9-13 with descriptions and benchmarks.

### [lectica-fischer] Dr. Kurt Fischer — Skill theory

Lectica's page on the theoretical foundation, explaining Fischer's contribution and how the Lectical Scale builds on his work.
